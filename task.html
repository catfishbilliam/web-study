Debrief

This study examines how people evaluate the accuracy of information and how personality traits and levels of trust may influence those judgments. Some items can depict true or false claims; any false items (when used) are drawn from fact-checking archives (e.g., Snopes, Reuters Fact Check, PolitiFact) and are included only for research purposes.

Epistemic mistrust refers to a general reluctance to accept information from others as credible (e.g., institutions, experts, or media). Understanding how mistrust relates to accuracy judgments can inform approaches to media literacy and misinformation resilience.
